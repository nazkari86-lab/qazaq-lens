# Qazaq Lens editorial guide

## Purpose

Write concise, source-visible context for international readers. The goal is not to defend Kazakhstan from every criticism. The goal is to distinguish checkable reality from fiction, shorthand and unsupported generalization.

## Voice

- Calm, specific and non-accusatory.
- Explain why a misconception occurs before correcting it.
- Never speak for all Kazakhstanis.
- Never replace one stereotype with promotional perfection.
- Prefer “the available evidence supports…” over “everyone knows…”.

## Claim workflow

1. State the misconception in one sentence.
2. Break the response into independently checkable claims.
3. Assign every substantial claim a stable `C` ID.
4. Add at least two relevant sources from genuinely independent publishers.
5. Record source type, publisher, access date and language.
6. Label the claim as fact, interpretation, reported view or disputed.
7. Assign high, medium or low confidence and explain uncertainty in the public text when material.
8. Cite the claim ID next to the sentence it supports.
9. Keep the article in public beta until a real external reviewer is recorded.

## Source selection

Prefer original legal texts, original data, primary statements, peer-reviewed scholarship and established reporting. A source is used only for what it can establish. Official sources establish laws, official positions and official data; they are not automatically neutral arbiters of disputed history or politics.

Do not count search snippets, AI output, anonymous posts, fan translations or copied aggregations as evidence. Social posts can document that a particular account made a statement, but not that the statement is true or representative.

## Sensitive subjects

Ethnicity, religion, minority experience, repression, conflict and identity terminology require an additional reviewer with relevant expertise or lived context before publication as reviewed. Avoid using a single interview as proof of a community-wide view.

## Corrections

Correct factual errors promptly. Add substantive changes to the public changelog. Do not silently rewrite material conclusions. Do not advance `lastReviewedAt` unless sources and wording were actually rechecked.

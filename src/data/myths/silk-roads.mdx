## The answer in 20 seconds

Kazakhstan was part of the Silk Roads network. UNESCO's transnational Chang'an–Tianshan corridor includes sites in Kazakhstan, China and Kyrgyzstan.<sup><a href="#claim-C1">C1</a></sup>

## A network, not a single road

The phrase “Silk Road” is a modern shorthand for many routes that shifted over time. Traders, pilgrims, diplomats, craftspeople and ideas moved through different valleys and passes depending on politics, climate and local power. The network connected rather than simply transported goods from one endpoint to another.

## Kazakhstan's place in the corridor

UNESCO describes routes through the Tian Shan and the Zhetysu region, including archaeological and historic sites in modern Kazakhstan. Akyrtas near Taraz is one of the recognised components. The evidence is not that every kilometre of Kazakhstan was a marketplace; it is that important routes and settlements crossed the region.<sup><a href="#claim-C2">C2</a></sup>

## Better wording

Say **"Kazakhstan contains important Silk Roads corridors and sites"**, not **"the Silk Road bypassed Kazakhstan."**<sup><a href="#claim-C3">C3</a></sup>

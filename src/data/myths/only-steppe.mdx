## The answer in 20 seconds

Steppe and desert are major parts of Kazakhstan, but the country also contains mountains, lakes, wetlands, rivers, forests and large cities.<sup><a href="#claim-C1">C1</a></sup>

## Why does the flat-steppe image dominate?

Kazakhstan is strongly associated with the Eurasian steppe, and large areas are genuinely arid or semi-arid. A true feature becomes a stereotype when it is presented as the only feature.

## What else is there?

The south and east include major mountain regions connected to the Tien Shan and Altai systems. Northern protected areas include steppe, freshwater and saltwater lakes, and globally important wetlands. UNESCO recognizes both Saryarka's steppe-and-lake ecosystems and the Western Tien-Shan mountain landscape.<sup><a href="#claim-C2">C2</a></sup>

## Why scale matters

Kazakhstan is the ninth-largest country by area. A single landscape description cannot represent a territory of that scale. Climate and topography differ sharply between northern plains, western deserts, central uplands, eastern mountains and the south-east around Almaty.<sup><a href="#claim-C3">C3</a></sup>

## Better wording

Say **“Kazakhstan includes vast steppe and desert regions”**, not “Kazakhstan is only steppe and desert.” The first is accurate; the second turns one part of the country into the whole country.

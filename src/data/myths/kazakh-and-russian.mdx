## The answer in 20 seconds

Kazakh and Russian are not dialects of one language. Kazakh is a Kipchak Turkic language; Russian belongs to the Indo-European family.<sup><a href="#claim-C1">C1</a></sup>

## Why are both heard in Kazakhstan?

Kazakhstan has a multilingual history and a large Russian-speaking population. Many people use Kazakh, Russian or both depending on family, region, education and workplace.

The legal roles are also different: Kazakh is the state language, while Russian has an officially defined role in public institutions.<sup><a href="#claim-C2">C2</a></sup>

## What about the alphabet?

Languages can share a writing system and still be unrelated. English and Vietnamese both use Latin-based alphabets; that does not make them the same language. The same logic applies here. Kazakh and Russian have both been written in Cyrillic in Kazakhstan, but their grammar, core vocabulary, sound systems and linguistic ancestry differ.<sup><a href="#claim-C3">C3</a></sup>

## Better phrasing

Say **“Kazakhstan is multilingual”** rather than “everyone speaks Russian” or “Kazakh is Russian.” Avoid assuming a person's preferred language from their ethnicity, city or appearance.

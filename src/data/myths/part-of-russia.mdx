## The answer in 20 seconds

Kazakhstan is not a Russian region. It is a sovereign state with its own constitution and membership in the United Nations.<sup><a href="#claim-C1">C1</a></sup>

## Why does the confusion happen?

Kazakhstan was one of the republics of the Soviet Union, uses Russian widely, shares a very long border with Russia and maintains extensive economic and cultural ties. Those facts can explain the confusion. They do not change the country's legal status.

## What changed in 1991?

Kazakhstan declared independence in December 1991 as the Soviet Union dissolved. It later entered the United Nations as a separate member state.<sup><a href="#claim-C2">C2</a></sup>

## Does Russian influence make it part of Russia?

No. Countries can share language, infrastructure, history, trade and security arrangements without one becoming a province of the other. Kazakhstan and Russia are neighbouring states with close and sometimes complicated ties.<sup><a href="#claim-C3">C3</a></sup>

## More accurate wording

Use **“Kazakhstan, a country in Central Asia”** rather than “a Russian region” or “part of Russia.” When discussing Soviet history, distinguish the former Kazakh Soviet Socialist Republic from the independent Republic of Kazakhstan that exists today.

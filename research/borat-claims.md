# Claim registry — Borat article

Canonical public claim data lives in `src/data/myths/borat.mdx`. This file records the editorial rationale and follow-up work.

## C1 — fictional character
Two sources: Netflix catalogue description and Encyclopaedia Britannica. Low-dispute fact.

## C2 — fabricated cultural setting
Two independent academic analyses in *Slavic Review*: Dickie Wallace and Robert A. Saunders. This is an interpretation supported by scholarship, not a measurable claim that every viewer was misled.

## C3 — Romania filming location
The Guardian's contemporary report and RFE/RL's 2020 retrospective independently identify Romania/Glod.

## C4 — satire of American society using Kazakhstan as frame
Netflix describes the mockumentary device; RFE/RL describes the main satirical target while documenting the Kazakh frame. Keep wording as interpretation, not authorial mind-reading.

## C5 — official reaction in 2005–2006
The Guardian contemporaneously reported threats of legal action and image campaigns; RFE/RL later documented discouraged screenings. Avoid the categorical word “banned” for Kazakhstan because sources describe an informal/effective ban rather than a clear statutory ban.

## C6 — 2020 tourism campaign
Primary campaign video plus a Kazakhstan government report. Strong fact.

## C7 — mixed reported reactions
RFE/RL and The Diplomat interviewed or quoted different Kazakhstanis. This proves a documented range, not population proportions.

## C8 — basic state and language facts
Government information page plus presidential official site. Appropriate for constitutional/state facts; not used as evidence for contested political evaluation.
